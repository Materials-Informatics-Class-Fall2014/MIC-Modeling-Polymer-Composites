(Briefly described in Reference 1) $$ E = E1*v1 +E2*v2 $$ E is the effective modulus. E1 and E2 are moduli of the individual components and v1 and v2 are the volume fractions. This is a simple mixing rule for an iso-strain state. This model assumes that the reinforcement is aligned in a single direction and that each component experiences the same strain. Ruess later modified this formulations to create the following (Reference 2) $$ E = (E1*E2)/(E1*v1 + E2*v2) $$ These two simple models form upper and lower bounds on the elastic modulus as determined by Hill (Reference 4).
